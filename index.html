01 — About

From equations to edges — and back to language

I'm a PhD student in the Department of Computer Science and Engineering at IIT Gandhinagar, working in theoretical computer science — specifically on combinatorial algorithms for detecting induced subgraphs.

My path here wasn't a straight line. I did my B.Sc. in Mathematics at St. Xavier's College, Ahmedabad, then an M.Sc. in Mathematics at IIT Gandhinagar, where I found myself pulled toward the algorithmic side of the field — the point where a proof turns into something you can actually run. That pull is what brought me into a PhD.

Outside the department, I'm a core member of the Ahmedabad Literary Society and the outgoing Secretary of the Literary Club at IIT Gandhinagar. I think of research and writing as the same instinct pointed in two directions: both are about finding the structure underneath something that, at first, looks unruly.

02 — Research

Finding small patterns inside big graphs

current focus

My current work is on combinatorial algorithms for induced subgraph detection: given a large graph, how quickly can we tell whether it contains a specific small pattern — a path, a cycle, or another fixed shape — as an induced subgraph? I work with polynomial-based methods and exploit automorphism parity to design faster detection algorithms, with particular attention to patterns like P₇ and C₇.

master's thesis · 2023–24

Mathematical Structures in Data and Music

Semester I — Topological Data Analysis

I studied how single-linkage clustering behaves on high-dimensional data, implemented the clustering algorithms in Python, and applied them to real institutional datasets.

Semester II — Mathematics of Music

I explored how algorithmic methods can compute the rank and metrical pattern of Swara permutations in Rev-Colex order, and implemented several of these ranking algorithms in Python.
